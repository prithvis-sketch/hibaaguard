What is HIPAA?

HIPAA Regulations

HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) is U.S. federal law that protects patient health information — known as Protected Health Information (PHI).

  • Any detail that identifies a patient — name, face, voice, medical record, diagnosis — is PHI.
  • Sharing PHI publicly without written patient consent violates HIPAA, even accidentally.
  • Violations carry fines from $100 to $50,000 per violation, up to $1.9M annually.
  • Applies to all U.S. healthcare providers, clinics, med spas, dentists, and their business associates.
  • Social media posts, TikToks, and reels are not exempt — the law applies to any public disclosure.
